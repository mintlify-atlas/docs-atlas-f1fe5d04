MyDiary gives you complete control over who can see each diary entry. Choose from public, private, or friends-only sharing.

## Understanding visibility levels

Every diary entry has one of three visibility settings:

### Public entries

**Who can see it:** All your accepted friends

Public entries appear in the feeds of all users who have an accepted friendship with you. This is the default setting when creating a new entry.

<Note>
  Public entries are NOT visible to all MyDiary users - only to people who are your friends.
</Note>

### Private entries

**Who can see it:** Only you

Private entries are completely hidden from all other users. Use this setting for:
- Personal reflections you don't want to share
- Sensitive thoughts or experiences
- Draft entries you might share later

### Friends-only entries

**Who can see it:** Specific friends you select

Friends-only entries are only visible to the friends you explicitly choose. This gives you granular control over sharing.

## Setting visibility when creating entries

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write your entry">
    Fill in the text area with your diary entry content.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click the visibility dropdown">
    Look for the icon at the bottom of the entry form (defaults to a public icon) and click it to open the visibility menu.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your privacy level">
    Choose from three options:
    
    - **Publico** (Public) - Visible to all friends
    - **Privado** (Private) - Visible only to you
    - **Solo algunos amigos** (Only some friends) - Visible to selected friends
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select friends (if needed)">
    If you chose "Solo algunos amigos", a modal will open showing your friend list.
    
    - Check the box next to each friend who should see this entry
    - Click **Listo** (Done) to confirm
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish">
    Click **Publicar entrada** to save your entry with the selected visibility.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Changing visibility on existing entries

You can update the visibility of any entry you've already published:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your entry">
    Locate the entry in your feed that you want to modify.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the edit modal">
    Click the edit icon (pencil) in the top-right corner of the entry card.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Change visibility">
    Click the visibility dropdown and select a new privacy level.
    
    <Tip>
      When changing to "Friends-only", you can select different friends than you originally chose.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save changes">
    Click **Publicar entrada** to update the entry with the new visibility setting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Changing an entry from public to private will immediately hide it from all friends. Changing from private to public will make it visible to all friends.
</Warning>

## Viewing shared entries in your feed

Your home feed displays entries from multiple sources:

1. **Your own entries** (all visibility levels)
2. **Public entries** from your friends
3. **Friends-only entries** where you were specifically selected

Entries are sorted by creation date, with the newest at the top.

## Visual indicators

Each entry shows a small icon indicating its visibility:

| Icon | Visibility Level |
|------|------------------|
| Globe/public icon | Public - All friends can see |
| Lock icon | Private - Only you can see |
| People icon | Friends-only - Selected friends can see |

## Best practices for sharing

<Tip>
  **Use friends-only for couple or close friend sharing**
  
  Create intimate moments by sharing entries with your partner or a small group of close friends. This is perfect for:
  - Personal updates you want to share with specific people
  - Couple's diary entries
  - Shared experiences with a particular friend group
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Start with private, share later**
  
  Write entries as private first, then edit them to make them public or friends-only once you're comfortable sharing.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Mix visibility levels**
  
  You don't have to use the same visibility for all entries. Mix public, private, and friends-only to create a rich, nuanced diary experience.
</Tip>
